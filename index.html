'R'-Value: The R value signifies how much co-relation exists b/w two seprate datas. If R>0.7 or R<-0.7: strong correlation, 0.7>R>0.4 or similarly in negative, moderate correlation, and any other value means weak correlation.

'P'-Value: The P value signifies if the correlation even exists at all, if p>0.05, the correlation is almost worthless.

Hence, from the above table it can be seen that during pre-lockdown and Ist and IInd lockdown, SENSEX and covid had the most correlation, even though during the Ist and IInd lockdown the movement was of the opposite trend than what might have been expected, but there was significant strong correlation between both.

But, in the month of May, SENSEX was able to stabilise against rising Covid trends, and no significant correlation can be seen as the p value is much higher than the applicable value.
